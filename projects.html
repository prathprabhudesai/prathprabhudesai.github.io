North Carolina State University (Course Projects)

Mixed 802.11n/802.11b/802.11g WLAN, RTS/CTS and Jammer Performance (report)
CSC/ECE 570: Computer Networks, Fall 2015

  • Performance issues of WLAN 802.11n, 802.11g and 802.11b based on network load, throughput and traffic drop.
  • Hidden node problem.
  • Network performance in presence of Jammer based on jammer location, in terms of drop in traffic and throughput.

M/M/1 Queue Simulation Modelling (report)
CSC/ECE 570: Computer Networks, Fall 2015

  • Modelled an M/M/1 Queueing system in OPNET and analysed the network performance based on packet arrival rates, packet sizes and service capacities.

Small Internetworks (report)
CSC/ECE 570: Computer Networks, Fall 2015

  • Star and extended-star network topologies were modelled for small office network using OPNET Riverbed Modeler.
  • Discussed performance issues related to Ethernet delay, Ethernet load and network traffic.

Stattistical Modelling of Fish Population (report)
ECE 514: Random Processes, Fall 2015

  • Echo SONAR is used for the determination of fish population which used sinusoidal sound pulses with rayleigh distributed amplitudes.
  • The school of fish was found to be normally distributed (gaussian) verifying the Central Limit Theorem.
  • The process was modelled using fish's position, orientation and motion as random variables.


Central Water and Power Research Station (Research Projects)

Flow Pattern and Velocity Measurement in a Physical Hydraulic Model Using Image Acquiring and Processing System (report)
Senior Year Thesis

  • System aims at open-stream water velocity profiling using Digital Image Processing algorithms influcned by motion tracking and Particle Image Velocimetry.
  • Research was carried out on Bombay Port Model to tackle the safety issues regarding over-flooding in the area of Navi Mumbai International Airport.
  • Then existing hardware technologies mainly included Vertical Acoustic Doppler Current Profiler (ADCP) sensors which are costly and fail to acheive directional profiling.
  • This non-contact technology provided a low cost and accurate solution overcoming the existing drawbacks.

Sediment Deposition Analysis: Categorization and Quantization

  • Research was carried out on the sediment depositories in the coastal hydraulic model with an aim of identifying the reservoir storage capacity, advanced planning for water management and strengthening silt removal process.
  • A new algorithm based on gamma correction was developed for the accurate detection of sediments and neural network techniques were used for the classification.

Bed Profiling and Dispersion Analysis

  • Analysis of topographic nature of the water channel was done using bed profiling with the help of sensor mapping and contour plotting.
  • The dispersion coefficients were calculated for the analysis of behavior of foreign fluids in water.
